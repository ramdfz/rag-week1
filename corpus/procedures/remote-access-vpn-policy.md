# Remote Access and VPN Policy

**Owner:** Information Technology
**Applies to:** All employees accessing company systems from outside a company facility

## 1. When VPN Is Required

1.1. Any connection to internal systems (file shares, internal applications, administrative portals) from outside a company facility.
1.2. Any use of company Wi-Fi guest networks or public Wi-Fi for company business.
1.3. Access to systems containing client, patient, or employee personal data under any circumstances outside the office.

## 2. When VPN Is Not Required

2.1. Access to company email or calendar through the approved mobile app, which uses its own encrypted channel.
2.2. Access to public-facing company websites or marketing materials.

## 3. Setup

3.1. New employees receive VPN credentials and a client installation guide during Day One IT onboarding; see the New Hire Onboarding Checklist.
3.2. VPN access requires multi-factor authentication in addition to the standard account password, per the IT Security and Acceptable Use Policy.
3.3. VPN client software must be kept on the current version; the client will prompt for update and block connection after 30 days on an outdated version.

## 4. Session Rules

4.1. VPN sessions automatically time out after 4 hours of inactivity.
4.2. Do not share VPN credentials or connect from a device that is not company-issued or MDM-enrolled.
4.3. Split-tunneling (routing only some traffic through VPN) is disabled by default and may not be re-enabled without IT Security approval.

## 5. Troubleshooting and Support

5.1. Connection issues should be reported to the IT service desk with the error code shown, if any.
5.2. Persistent authentication failures may indicate account compromise; do not repeatedly retry more than 3 times before contacting IT Security.

## 6. Offboarding

6.1. VPN access is revoked immediately upon termination or resignation as part of standard offboarding, coordinated with People Operations.
