# New Hire Onboarding Checklist

**Owner:** People Operations
**Applies to:** All new employees, Meridian Health Partners

## 1. Before Day One

1.1. HR sends the offer confirmation and start date in writing.
1.2. IT provisions a company email account and laptop, ready for pickup or shipping by end of business the Friday before the start date.
1.3. The hiring manager assigns an onboarding buddy from the new hire's team.
1.4. Facilities issues a building access badge, activated for the start date.

## 2. Day One

2.1. New hire reports to People Operations at 9:00 AM for orientation.
2.2. Complete Form I-9 and W-4 (or state equivalent) in person with a People Operations representative.
2.3. Review and electronically sign: Code of Conduct Policy, Anti-Harassment Policy, Remote Work Policy (if applicable), and the Data Handling and Confidentiality Guidelines.
2.4. IT walks the new hire through account activation, multi-factor authentication setup, and VPN configuration per the Remote Access and VPN Policy.
2.5. Benefits enrollment window opens; see the Benefits Enrollment Walkthrough for enrollment steps and deadlines.

## 3. First Week

3.1. Meet with direct manager to review role expectations and 30/60/90-day goals.
3.2. Complete mandatory compliance training modules: workplace safety, anti-harassment, and data privacy.
3.3. Onboarding buddy schedules informal introductions with core team members.
3.4. New hire confirms receipt of all equipment and reports any issues to IT.

## 4. First 30 Days

4.1. Manager conducts a 30-day check-in covering early feedback and open questions.
4.2. People Operations confirms benefits enrollment was completed within the required window.
4.3. New hire completes any department-specific systems training.

## 5. Escalation

5.1. Questions about pay, benefits, or policy should go to People Operations, not the hiring manager.
5.2. IT equipment or access issues should be logged through the IT service desk, not handled informally.
