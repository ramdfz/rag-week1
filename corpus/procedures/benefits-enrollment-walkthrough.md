# Benefits Enrollment Walkthrough

**Owner:** People Operations
**Applies to:** New hires and employees during Open Enrollment

## 1. When You Can Enroll

1.1. New hires have 30 days from their start date to make initial elections.
1.2. All employees may change elections during the annual Open Enrollment period, typically held in November.
1.3. Outside these windows, changes are only permitted following a qualifying life event (marriage, birth or adoption of a child, loss of other coverage), reported within 30 days of the event.

## 2. Medical Plan Selection

2.1. Review the plan summaries available in the benefits portal, including deductible, out-of-pocket maximum, and network details for each option.
2.2. Compare plan tiers (employee only, employee plus spouse, employee plus children, family) and confirm dependent eligibility documentation is ready to upload if adding dependents.
2.3. Note whether a plan requires a primary care physician referral for specialists before enrolling, if that matters to your household.

## 3. Retirement Plan

3.1. Enrollment in the 401(k) plan is available starting on day one of employment.
3.2. Review the Summary Plan Description for details on employer matching, vesting schedule, and investment options before setting a contribution percentage.
3.3. Auto-escalation (annual automatic contribution increase) can be opted into or out of during enrollment.

## 4. Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

4.1. EAP coverage is automatic for all employees at no cost and does not require enrollment.
4.2. Review the EAP overview document for the services covered and how to access them confidentially.

## 5. Completing Enrollment

5.1. Submit all elections through the benefits portal before the enrollment deadline; late submissions default to no coverage until the next enrollment window, except where a qualifying life event applies.
5.2. A confirmation summary is emailed after submission; review it for accuracy and report discrepancies to People Operations within 5 business days.

## 6. Questions

6.1. Plan-specific coverage questions should go to the carrier directly using the contact information in the plan summary.
6.2. Enrollment process or eligibility questions should go to People Operations.
