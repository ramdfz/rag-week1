# Incident Reporting Procedure

**Owner:** Environmental Health and Safety, in coordination with People Operations
**Applies to:** All employees at Meridian Health Partners facilities

## 1. What Counts as a Reportable Incident

1.1. Any workplace injury, regardless of severity.
1.2. Any near-miss where injury could reasonably have occurred.
1.3. Property damage exceeding $500.
1.4. Any incident involving a client, patient, or visitor on company premises.
1.5. Any suspected data privacy or security incident (see the IT Security and Acceptable Use Policy for related reporting).

## 2. Immediate Steps

2.1. Ensure the safety of all individuals involved before anything else.
2.2. Administer first aid if trained and safe to do so; call emergency services for anything beyond minor first aid.
2.3. Do not move injured persons unless there is an immediate danger requiring evacuation.
2.4. Secure the area if equipment or a hazard is involved, to prevent a second incident.

## 3. Reporting Timeline

3.1. Verbal notification to the direct supervisor or on-site safety lead within 30 minutes of the incident.
3.2. Written incident report submitted through the incident reporting system within 24 hours.
3.3. For incidents involving a client or patient, People Operations and the compliance team must be notified within 4 hours regardless of severity.

## 4. What Goes in the Written Report

4.1. Date, time, and precise location of the incident.
4.2. Names of all individuals involved and any witnesses.
4.3. A factual description of what happened, without speculation about fault.
4.4. Any immediate corrective action already taken.
4.5. Photographs of the scene or damage, where safe and appropriate to take.

## 5. Investigation and Follow-Up

5.1. Environmental Health and Safety reviews all reports within 3 business days.
5.2. Incidents meeting the OSHA recordability threshold are logged on the OSHA 300 log by the designated recordkeeper.
5.3. Root-cause findings and corrective actions are shared with the affected department within 10 business days.

## 6. Non-Retaliation

6.1. Employees will not face retaliation for reporting an incident in good faith, including near-misses.
6.2. Failure to report a known incident within the required timeline may itself be subject to review under the Code of Conduct Policy.
