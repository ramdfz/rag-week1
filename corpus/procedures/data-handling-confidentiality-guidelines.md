# Data Handling and Confidentiality Guidelines

**Owner:** Compliance, in coordination with Information Technology
**Applies to:** All employees who access client, patient, or employee data

## 1. Data Classification

1.1. **Restricted** — health information, Social Security numbers, financial account details. Requires encryption at rest and in transit, and access is limited to employees with a documented business need.
1.2. **Internal** — company policies, internal communications, org charts. Not for external sharing without approval.
1.3. **Public** — marketing materials, published job postings. No restrictions on sharing.

## 2. Handling Restricted Data

2.1. Restricted data may only be stored in approved company systems, never on personal devices or personal cloud storage, per the IT Security and Acceptable Use Policy.
2.2. Restricted data may not be emailed externally without encryption, and only with a documented business justification.
2.3. Printed materials containing restricted data must be stored in a locked location when not in active use and shredded, not discarded in regular trash, when no longer needed.
2.4. Screens displaying restricted data must be locked when the employee steps away, even briefly.

## 3. Access Principles

3.1. Access to restricted data is granted on a need-to-know basis tied to job function, not seniority.
3.2. Access is reviewed quarterly by data owners; unused access is revoked.
3.3. Any request for data access outside an employee's normal job function must be approved in writing by the data owner and Compliance.

## 4. Third Parties

4.1. Sharing any restricted or internal data with a vendor, contractor, or partner requires a signed data processing agreement in place first.
4.2. Employees may not verify a third party's identity or authorization informally (for example, over a phone call alone) before sharing restricted data; use the documented verification process.

## 5. Incident Response

5.1. Any suspected exposure of restricted data, however minor, must be reported immediately per the Incident Reporting Procedure.
5.2. Do not attempt to independently investigate or contain a suspected data incident; escalate to IT Security and Compliance first.

## 6. Retention and Disposal

6.1. Data is retained only as long as required by business need or applicable regulation, per the retention schedule maintained by Compliance.
6.2. Disposal of restricted data, physical or digital, must use an approved secure deletion method, not standard delete or discard.
